My Personal History

Howdy and good day! I am Juan Duran and this is my story.

Beginnings

I was the first of three children born to first-generation Americans and up until I five years old, we lived in the garage my father rented from my grandfather. All my life, academics was heavily pushed by my family and drove me to graduate third in my class from Chester W. Nimitz High School and allowed me to earn enough scholarships to afford college.

College Part I

I entered Texas A&M's College of Engineering as an aspiring nuclear engineering student. I quickly involved myself with peer teaching, supplemental instruction, and personal tutoring for the freshmen engineering program to both pay for college and give me a way to assist others in overcoming struggles I experienced in previous courses. I very quickly rose to the senior ranks of the peer teachers and was promoted to the lead supplemental instructor for the program.

Pre-flight testing before first simulated zero-G flight at Ellington Airfield

I also had the opportunity to participate with A&M's Nuclear Power Institute and work on a research project with NASA in hardware/software engineering role. It was through these experience that I discovered my fascination with programing, design, and electronics, topics I had at this point been teaching students but not using in my major. I ultimately decided to switch into computer engineering starting my senior year and began my next stage of life.

College Part II

It was in these next three years that I would involve myself with the Society of Women Engineers; it was through my many students I previously had taught inviting me to numerous events with a plethora of food. I made many lasting friendships and networked with many visiting speakers. In addition this, I decided to make use of the extra time I was spending in college to minor in Cybersecurity and Mathematics. Finally, after a total of five and a half years, I completed my college adventure and could not be happier.

Post-Graduation

I spent another five months continuing work at Texas A&M to assist the Academic Success Center and the freshmen engineering program to cycle into the new course curriculum that was developed. During this time, I was active job hunting but to no avail. It was not until late in August that I was contacted by Talent Path and ultimately began my role as a junior full stack engineer in September.

Personal Life

I met my wife in 5th grade and we later started dating sophomore year of high school. We both attended Texas A&M with her joining as a computer engineering major and graduating on time with honors. She was a major source for inspiration for me to ultimately change majors and she supported me through my five and a half year ordeal there.

Hobbies

In my free time I enjoy playing the Star Wars Roleplaying Game. I am my group's game master and for our most recent campaign we've had a consistent session every other week for the past three months. In addition to this, I also play Star Wars Armada, a Star Wars tabletop game, with the local Houston area group every week. This has been a more recent hobby I've acquired and I'm aspiring to qualify for the international championships.
