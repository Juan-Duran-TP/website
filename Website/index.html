My Personal History

Howdy and good day! I am Juan Duran and this is my story.

Beginnings

I was the first of three children born to first-generation Americans and for the first five years of my life, we lived in the garage my father rented from my grandfather. All my life, academics was heavily pushed by my family and drove me to graduate third in my class from Chester W. Nimitz High School and have enough scholarships to afford college.

College Part I

I entered Texas A&M's College of Engineering as an aspiring nuclear engineering student. I quickly got involved with peer teaching, supplemental instruction, and personal tutoring for the freshmen engineering program to both pay for college and give me a way to assist others in overcoming struggles I experienced in previous courses. I also involved myself with A&M's Nuclear Power Institute and had the opportunity to work on a research project with NASA in hardware and software role. It was through these experience that I discovered my fascination with programing, design, and electronics, topics I had at this point been teaching students but not using in my major. I ultimately decided to switch into computer engineering starting my senior year and began my next stage of life.

College Part II

It was in these next three years that I would involve myself with the Society of Women Engineers; it was through my many students I previously had taught inviting me to numerous events with a plethora of food. I made many lasting friendships and networked with many visiting speakers. In addition this, I decided to make use of the extra time I was spending in college to minor in Cybersecurity and Mathematics. Finally, after a total of five and a half years, I completed my college adventure and could not be happier.
